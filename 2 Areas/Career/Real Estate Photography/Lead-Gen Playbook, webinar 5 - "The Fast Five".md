


The **Fast Five** is an automated lead nurture "recipe" designed to solve the problem of slow lead response times by engaging new prospects within the first five minutes of lead generation [2][8].

### Core Workflow [2][9][10]

1. **Immediate Pipeline Entry**: The lead is automatically pushed into a sales pipeline to create an "opportunity" [2][9].
2. **Confirmation Email**: An automated email is sent immediately, typically confirming a promotion or offer the lead claimed [2][9].
3. **Conversational SMS**: A text message is sent (00:10:25) [9] that is designed to be brief and conversational to elicit a simple "yes" or "no" response (e.g., "Is now a good time to get you scheduled?") [10].
4. **AI Response Handling**: The system waits two minutes for a reply (00:10:51) [10]. If the lead responds, an AI (leveraging Google's Dialogflow) determines if the intent is positive or negative [10].
    - **Positive**: The system automatically sends a booking link [10].
    - **Negative/No Reply**: The lead follows a different path, such as being moved to a long-term nurture [10].

### Advanced Engagement Steps [12]

If the initial automated steps do not result in a booking, the recipe can trigger more direct actions:

- **Call Bridging**: The system calls the client's front desk and, upon a keypress, automatically dials the lead to connect them instantly (00:13:14) [12].
- **Voicemail Drops**: If the call is not answered, a pre-recorded "ringless" voicemail can be dropped into the lead's inbox (00:13:32) [12].

### Strategic Value [8][11]

The goal of the Fast Five is to increase conversion rates, which can jump by nearly **400%** if a lead is engaged within the first minute (00:06:45) [8]. It prevents "ugly conversations" with clients who might otherwise complain that leads are "stale" or "bad" simply because they weren't contacted quickly enough (00:04:05) [11].
