## Introduction to Real Estate Photography Marketing

- To land real estate photography clients, having a solid marketing strategy is crucial, and a simple step-by-step plan can help get gigs and turn them into long-term clients with repeat work [(00:00:00)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=0s).
- The key to this plan is to show legitimacy by taking deliberate actions and deliberate steps in the proper order, rather than relying on going viral, cold calling, or spamming [(00:00:21)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=21s).

## Building a Real Estate Photography Portfolio

- The first step is to do some prep work, which includes creating a simple portfolio with about 10 really good photos that showcase the ability to shoot good real estate photos, including interiors like kitchens and bathrooms, as well as twilight photos [(00:01:06)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=66s).
- To overcome the chicken and egg paradox of needing a portfolio to get gigs but not being able to build a portfolio without gigs, it's essential to practice shooting at home, composing, and editing to get really good and be able to stand out [(00:02:05)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=125s).
- Besides shooting at home, practicing with friends' newly remodeled rooms or properties can also help build a portfolio, and it's important to remember that the portfolio will grow over time, so only a few good photos are needed initially [(00:02:42)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=162s).
- Once about 10 really good photos are collected, it's time to move on to the next steps, and for those who need to improve their real estate photography skills, online courses and books are available [(00:03:19)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=199s).

## Establishing a Professional Online Presence

- To establish a professional presence, it is essential to have a simple website dedicated solely to real estate photography, which can be just one page and include a portfolio, conveying professionalism and separating it from other types of photography work [(00:03:43)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=223s).
- Creating an Instagram page dedicated to real estate photography is also crucial, as it helps to show seriousness about the work and should not be mixed with other types of photos, such as family portraits [(00:04:29)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=269s).
- Making a QR code that links to the website is a necessary step, as it allows people to easily access the portfolio and information about the services, and this QR code can be customized using tools like Adobe Express [(00:05:10)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=310s).
- The QR code should be placed on a simple business card, which can be ordered at a low cost from websites like Vista Print, and having business cards is important for handing out to potential clients [(00:05:48)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=348s).

## Setting Prices and Pricing Strategy

- Before engaging with clients, it is vital to establish a price list, but this should not be made publicly available on the website, as the goal is to attract clients based on the quality of the work, not just the price [(00:06:27)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=387s).
- Setting the right price requires research and consideration of the costs in the area, and there are additional resources available, such as a separate video on pricing real estate photography, which can provide guidance on how to determine the optimal prices [(00:06:56)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=416s).

## Targeted Marketing and Finding Potential Clients

- To find potential clients for real estate photography, it is essential to have targeted marketing and know who to work with, rather than spamming everybody or relying on viral marketing, which gives a better chance of working with quality clients [(00:07:36)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=456s).
- Using websites like realtor.com, which is recommended for those in the United States, allows for searching houses in a specific area and finding listing agents, while avoiding websites like Zillow that favor paying agents [(00:08:01)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=481s).
- Looking for listings with low-quality photos provides an edge, as it allows for offering services to improve the photos, and finding the listing agent's profile on realtor.com helps determine their experience and sales history [(00:08:28)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=508s).
- Recording the listing agent's name, email address, and property address is necessary for crafting an email, and this process should be done for about five agents initially to start the outreach process [(00:09:14)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=554s).

## Timing and Seasonal Considerations for Outreach

- It is crucial to reach out to clients at the right time, typically during the spring, summer, and fall when realtors are getting listings and need photographers, and avoiding the slow months during the winter holidays [(00:10:07)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=607s).
- Using the slow months for prep work, such as practicing, gathering information, building a website, and creating business cards, gives an advantage and allows for a more effective outreach process when the busy season starts [(00:10:32)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=632s).
- In the United States and most of Europe, the best time to start reaching out to realtors for real estate photography services is around March, when spring begins, and the real estate market becomes more active [(00:10:50)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=650s).

## Crafting and Sending Outreach Emails

- To reach out to realtors, it's essential to construct a personalized email with a subject line such as "photography for your listing" and include the property address, an introduction, and an offer of 50% off the first shoot, along with examples of work and contact information [(00:11:15)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=675s).
- It's recommended to wait about a week after sending the email to one realtor, and if there's no response, move on to the next realtor on the list, rather than offering freebie shoots, which can undermine confidence in one's work [(00:11:55)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=715s).
- During the waiting period, it's crucial to practice and refine skills, work on the website and social media, and reach out to friends for potential shoot opportunities to keep refining the process [(00:12:30)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=750s).

## Handling Non-Responses and Refining the Approach

- If there's no response from the first five realtors, it may be necessary to reassess the approach, and consider reaching out to more realtors or attending local open houses to network and distribute business cards [(00:13:17)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=797s).
- Remembering that it only takes one client to get referrals is vital, as real estate photography work is often visible to potential clients through listing sites, and high-quality photos can lead to referrals from other real estate agents [(00:13:45)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=825s).
- Attending local open houses can be an effective way to network and distribute business cards, although it can be a tricky proposition, and it's essential to be prepared and confident in one's services [(00:14:34)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=874s).

## Networking at Open Houses

- To approach real estate agents, it is best to visit open houses towards the end of the scheduled time, around 3:30 p.m. if the open house is from 1:00 p.m. to 4:00 p.m., to avoid being a nuisance and to have a more productive conversation with the agent [(00:15:13)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=913s).
- When meeting the realtor, it is essential to have a business card ready, introduce yourself as a real estate photographer, and compliment them on their open house, while keeping the conversation short and letting them know that you are local to the area and available to help with real estate photography [(00:15:28)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=928s).
- It is crucial to be respectful of the realtor's time and not take a tour of the house, as the goal is to show that you are efficient and respectful of their time [(00:15:56)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=956s).

## Preparation and Follow-Up for Networking

- Before visiting open houses, it is recommended to already have some clients in the queue, possibly from targeted emails, and if there are no responses from these efforts, it may be necessary to re-evaluate your approach and consider seeking feedback from someone who knows the industry [(00:16:11)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=971s).
- If emailing and visiting open houses are not yielding any responses, it may be necessary to step back and assess what could be going wrong, whether it be the portfolio, Instagram, or personal confidence, and consider seeking remote photography coaching or feedback from someone in the real estate industry [(00:17:07)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=1027s).

## Avoiding Common Mistakes in Outreach

- It is important to avoid blasting emails to a large list of real estate agents, as this can lead to a negative reputation if mistakes are made, and instead, focus on targeted and personalized approaches to build relationships and grow your business [(00:17:54)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=1074s).
- To get the first clients for real estate photography, it is essential to research and create a list of around five to ten realtors in the area, and then start emailing and reaching out to them, which should eventually lead to some type of response and potentially gigs [(00:18:27)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=1107s).

## Securing First Clients and Building a Client Base

- Getting one gig and providing excellent service and standout photos can help gain referrals and establish a client base, which is crucial for long-term success in real estate photography [(00:18:42)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=1122s).
- The key to successfully getting clients is to approach the process with intention, doing research on the desired clients, and understanding that it is more than just getting gigs, but rather about building a client base that can provide repeat work [(00:18:55)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=1135s).
- Establishing long-term clients can lead to a steady stream of work and reduce the need for constant marketing, allowing a professional real estate photographer to build a sustainable business over many years [(00:19:16)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=1156s).
- Building a strong relationship with clients and providing excellent service can lead to repeat work and referrals, making it easier to grow a real estate photography business and achieve long-term success [(00:19:25)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eDNrpB2ibv8&t=1165s).
