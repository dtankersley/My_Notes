

## Common Mistakes in Real Estate Photography

- The five big mistakes that photographers make when they first get into real estate photography can cost them clients and repeat work if not corrected, with the fifth mistake being not thinking like a buyer, which involves concentrating on the technical aspects more than the context of the photos [(00:00:19)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKXzhs9iRqY&t=19s).
- Concentrating on technical aspects such as f-stops, white balance, and exposure is important, but it is more crucial to capture photos that will sell the house, which requires thinking like a home buyer and understanding what will attract them to the property [(00:00:43)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKXzhs9iRqY&t=43s).

## Composition and Lens Choice

- Composing photos properly is essential, and this involves shooting wide enough to showcase the space, but not so wide that the features of the room are lost, with the goal of finding a happy medium that accurately represents the property [(00:01:39)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKXzhs9iRqY&t=99s).
- Using an ultra-wide angle lens can be misleading, as it can make small spaces appear larger than they are, and it is better to use a variable length lens, such as a 14 to 24 or 16 to 35, which provides more flexibility in different spaces [(00:02:40)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKXzhs9iRqY&t=160s).

## Capturing Selling Features

- Capturing the selling features of a house is critical, and this includes showcasing the critical counters in a kitchen, such as new sinks, microwaves, and appliances, which are important details that home buyers want to see [(00:03:23)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKXzhs9iRqY&t=203s).
- Real estate photographers should ensure they capture the critical components that will help sell a property, rather than just focusing on making the space look good, and this requires a balance between technical applications and context [(00:03:42)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKXzhs9iRqY&t=222s).

## Lighting and Image Tone

- The poor use of light is a common mistake in real estate photography, and photographers should aim to create bright and airy images, following the mantras "airy over artsy" and "less drama and more definition" [(00:03:57)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKXzhs9iRqY&t=237s).
- When shooting for the listing market, photographers should avoid using the editorial style of architectural design, which can result in dark and dramatic images, and instead focus on creating light and bright images that will appeal to potential buyers [(00:04:27)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKXzhs9iRqY&t=267s).
- Many architectural design clients, including interior designers, want images that are light and bright, and will appeal to the consumer market, which is the same market that buys homes through the listing market [(00:05:44)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKXzhs9iRqY&t=344s).
- Real estate photographers should prioritize capturing what their clients want and expect, rather than focusing on creating dramatic images, and should aim to create images that are airy, bright, and well-defined [(00:06:18)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKXzhs9iRqY&t=378s).

## White Balance Consistency

- Inconsistent white balance is a common mistake in real estate photography, and photographers can learn how to get the right white balance every time by watching a video on the topic, which is linked in the description [(00:07:13)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKXzhs9iRqY&t=433s).
- Inconsistent white balance is a common amateur mistake in real estate photography, and it is crucial to keep the white balance consistent throughout the shoot to avoid giving the impression that different properties are being photographed, even if it means using a slightly incorrect white balance [(00:07:33)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKXzhs9iRqY&t=453s).
- Using auto white balance can lead to inconsistent white balance, as it may change when capturing different angles or close-ups, and it is essential to take control of the white balance and use the proper color temperature and tint for subsequent pictures of the same room or space [(00:08:00)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKXzhs9iRqY&t=480s).

## Alignment and Camera Positioning

- Vertical alignment is critical in real estate photography, and it is essential to keep verticals vertical and avoid converging lines, as well as ensuring that photos are not rotated, to achieve a professional look and avoid extension distortion [(00:09:00)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKXzhs9iRqY&t=540s).
- Horizontal alignment is also important, as being off by a little can result in extension distortion, which is particularly noticeable in wide-angle shots, and it is crucial to get the alignment right to achieve a straight-on shot with minimal distortion [(00:09:27)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKXzhs9iRqY&t=567s).
- Camera angles and height are also crucial in real estate photography, and it is essential to consider the rules of shooting specific rooms, such as kitchens and bathrooms, to ensure that all selling features are visible and that the camera is positioned at the right height to provide context [(00:10:20)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKXzhs9iRqY&t=620s).
- Sacrificing context to hide the camera or avoid reflections is not a good idea, and instead, photographers should use techniques such as the remove tool to remove the camera from mirrors and ensure that all selling features are visible, while also finding a balance between showing the features and avoiding an unflattering perspective [(00:10:46)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKXzhs9iRqY&t=646s).

## Editing and Skill Development

- The top mistake that amateur photographers make is outsourcing their editing too early, and instead, they should do their own editing to learn and strengthen their skills, especially when first starting out [(00:11:40)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKXzhs9iRqY&t=700s).
- By editing their own photos, photographers can learn what works and what doesn't, and this knowledge will help them to capture better footage on site, making the editing process faster and more efficient [(00:12:35)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKXzhs9iRqY&t=755s).
- Outsourcing editing can be necessary when photographers are too busy, but it's essential to outsource at the right time in their career, and not just because they can't edit, as this can hide weaknesses in their photography skills [(00:11:47)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKXzhs9iRqY&t=707s).
- Learning to edit photos properly can help photographers to grow in their career, especially if they want to move into higher-paying work such as architectural and design photography, which requires a high level of skill and collaboration with clients [(00:13:53)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKXzhs9iRqY&t=833s).
- Doing their own editing allows photographers to charge by the hour for their work, including shooting and editing, and to collaborate with clients in an iterative process, which can lead to more opportunities and higher earnings [(00:14:12)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKXzhs9iRqY&t=852s).
- Outsourcing editors may not have the necessary skills to edit architectural and design photos, which have higher standards and often require the use of lights, so it's crucial for photographers to learn how to edit these types of photos themselves before outsourcing [(00:14:45)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKXzhs9iRqY&t=885s).
- As photographers grow and become too busy, they can then outsource their editing to the right type of editor who can help them with the specific type of work they need, but only after they have developed their own editing skills [(00:15:00)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKXzhs9iRqY&t=900s).
