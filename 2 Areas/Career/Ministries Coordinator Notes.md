
Managing a large parish of 600 families is a major logistical feat. At $14,000 per year, you are essentially entering a "stipend-based" role that hinges entirely on how many hours you are expected to work.

Below is a summary of what you can expect from this specific role and the critical questions you should ask to ensure you aren't signing up for a 40-hour workload at a 10-hour pay rate.

### Summary of the Ministry Coordinator Role

1. **The "Bridge" Role:** You are the connective tissue between the Parish Council's vision and the congregation's action. You'll likely spend more time on "people management" than administrative paperwork.
    
2. **Recruitment Hub:** In a church of this size, you aren't just finding volunteers; you are building a "culture of service." You will be responsible for the "Ask," vetting (background checks), and retention of people across multiple ministries.
    
3. **Communication Management:** You will likely be the voice of the church, managing weekly emails (like MailChimp), social media, and "Connect Cards" for new families.
    
4. **Logistical Oversight:** This often includes Sunday operations—arriving early to ensure the "hospitality" and "check-in" teams are ready—and potentially managing space rentals or event setups.
    
5. **The Pay Gap:** Based on 2026 data, the average professional hourly rate for this role is **$21–$27/hr**. At **$14k/year**, a "fair" schedule for this pay is roughly **10–12 hours per week**. Anything over 15 hours moves you into a "below-market" territory.
    
6. **Staff Dynamics:** Since there is already an office staff, your role is likely to "offload" the relational work (volunteers) so the office staff can focus on the business/operations side.

### Critical Interview Questions

Because your hours are currently "unknown," these questions are designed to flush out the true scope of the job so you don't get burned out.

#### **On Hours & Logistics**

- "For a $14,000 annual stipend, **how many hours per week** have you budgeted for this position? Is there a cap on those hours?"
    
- "Is this role expected to be **primarily on-site** during office hours, or is it mostly focused on **Sunday mornings** and remote mid-week prep?"
    
- "How are **seasonal spikes** handled? For example, during Christmas or Easter, will I be expected to work 30 hours for the same pay, or is there a 'flex time' arrangement?"
    

#### **On Volunteer Recruitment**

- "With 600 families, which **specific ministries** will I be recruiting for? Is it _all_ of them, or just a few key areas like Sunday School and Ushers?"
    
- "What is the current **volunteer-to-family ratio**? Are we looking to maintain an existing team or rebuild one from scratch?"
    
- "Who handles the **administrative vetting** (background checks and 'Safe Environment' paperwork)—me, or the existing office staff?"
    

#### **On Reporting & Authority**

- "Who is my **direct supervisor**? In a church with a Parish Council and office staff, it's easy to have 'too many bosses'—who provides my final priority list?"
    
- "What **tools or software** does the church currently use for coordination (e.g., Planning Center, Slack, or Trello)? Will I be expected to implement new systems?"
    
- "What does **success look like** for this role one year from now?"
    

**Pro-Tip:** If they say the hours are "whatever it takes to get the job done," that is your cue to suggest a **10-hour weekly average.** In a large church, "whatever it takes" usually translates to "much more than 10 hours."
