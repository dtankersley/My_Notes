### 1. Master Your Craft First (Before Chasing Paid Gigs)
Don't waste agents' time with mediocre work. Practice relentlessly on your own place, friends' homes, or vacant listings (ask nicely or shoot model homes if accessible). Focus on:
- **Composition**: Wide angles that make rooms feel spacious but not distorted. Level horizons, straight verticals.
- **Lighting**: Learn **HDR bracketing** (shoot 3-5 exposures) and blend them. Use natural light + your existing skills; avoid harsh flash until you invest.
- **Exterior & Drone**: Golden hour or blue hour for exteriors. When you borrow the drone, nail cinematic shots and obey FAA rules (Part 107 cert if you go commercial—study for it free online; it's required for paid work).
- **Consistency**: Same style every time so agents know what to expect.

Shoot 20-30 "practice" properties until your portfolio looks pro. In SoCal, agents expect clean, bright, modern edits that pop on Zillow/Redfin/MLS—think bright whites, vibrant but natural colors, decluttered feel (remove trash cans, cars if possible in post).

**Pro Tip**: Study top local shooters' work on listings in your area (LA, Orange County, Inland Empire, etc.). Mimic the successful ones without copying.

- Target: Newer agents or those with budget listings who hate their phone photos.

This builds social proof fast.

### 6. Software on Linux (As the Need Arises)
Since you run Linux, stick to native/open-source tools that are powerful and free. No Adobe subscriptions needed early on.

**Core Editing Workflow**:
- **RAW Processing & Cataloging (Lightroom equivalent)**: Start with **Darktable** (my go-to recommendation—non-destructive, excellent modules for exposure, color, HDR merging, masking). It's actively developed and handles real estate needs well. Alternative: **RawTherapee** (great for technical quality, 32-bit processing).
- **Advanced Retouching/Compositing (Photoshop equivalent)**: **GIMP** with plugins (for sky replacements, object removal, blending). Learn luminosity masking or frequency separation for pro looks.
- **Organization**: Use Darktable's lighttable or simple file system + `exiftool` (command-line for metadata) + Digikam if you need a stronger DAM (digital asset management).

**Delivery & Client Tools**:
- Free cloud: Nextcloud (self-hosted on your machine) or Proton Drive/Mega for sharing galleries.
- Client management: Google Sheets or free tiers of Flowlu/Crm-like tools. Later, simple open-source options or Pixieset free tier for client galleries (web-based).
- Invoicing: LibreOffice or free Wave app (web).

**Learning**:
- YouTube tutorials for "Darktable real estate photography workflow" or "GIMP HDR real estate".
- As you grow and need speed: Consider AI tools (some web-based like free tiers of removal tools) or paid one-time options like Affinity Photo (runs decent via compatibility layers, but test on Linux).

Install via your distro's package manager (e.g., Flatpak/AppImage for easy updates). These will handle 90%+ of pro real estate work without subscriptions.
