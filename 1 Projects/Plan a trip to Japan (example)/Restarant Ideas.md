

## Tokyo

- **Ichiran Ramen** (Shibuya) — solo booth tonkotsu ramen, great for first night
- **Tsukiji Outer Market** — tamagoyaki, fresh sushi for breakfast
- **Omoide Yokocho** (Shinjuku) — tiny yakitori alleys, amazing atmosphere
- **Afuri** — yuzu shio ramen, lighter style

## Kyoto

- **Nishiki Market** — street food grazing: mochi, pickles, grilled skewers
- **Gion area** — look for small kaiseki places, ask the ryokan for recommendations

## Osaka

- **Dotonbori** — takoyaki (octopus balls) and okonomiyaki are a must
- **Kuromon Market** — fresh seafood, grilled wagyu skewers

## Tips

- Google Maps ratings are reliable in Japan
- Lunch sets (teishoku) are often half the price of dinner at the same restaurant
- Don't tip — it's considered rude
- "Sumimasen" (excuse me) goes a long way
