## The Author's Perspective on AI's Rapid Evolution
- The author of the document "Something Big Is Happening" reflects on how the world changed drastically in a short period, similar to what happened in February 2020 with the Covid-19 pandemic, and believes that something much bigger is currently unfolding, related to Artificial Intelligence (AI).
- The author, who has spent six years building an AI startup and investing in the space, feels that they have been downplaying the significance of AI developments to their family and friends, but can no longer keep quiet about the rapid progress being made.
- A small number of researchers at companies like [[OpenAI]], [[Anthropic]], Google DeepMind, and a few others are shaping the future of AI, with a single training run having the potential to produce an AI system that shifts the entire trajectory of the technology.
- The author notes that most people in the AI industry, including themselves, are building on top of foundations they did not lay and are watching the developments unfold, feeling the ground shake first.

## Personal Experience and Observations in the AI Industry
- The reason for the alarm being sounded by people in the industry is that the rapid progress of AI has already occurred in their own jobs, and they are warning others that they will be next.
- In 2025, new techniques for building AI models unlocked a much faster pace of progress, with each new model being better than the last by a wider margin, and the time between new model releases getting shorter.
- The author shares their personal experience of using AI, which has reached a point where they can describe what they want built in plain English, and the AI can produce the finished product without needing any corrections or edits.
- The author provides an example of how they can now simply describe the outcome they want to the AI, and it will be done, without needing any guidance or corrections, highlighting the significant capabilities of the current AI systems.
- The latest AI model, GPT-5.3 Codex, has demonstrated the ability to write tens of thousands of lines of code, test and refine its own work, and even make intelligent decisions, which is a significant improvement from previous models.
- This new model has the capacity to iterate and refine its work until it meets its own standards, and when tested by the author, it is usually perfect, which is a remarkable development in the field of artificial intelligence.

## The Accelerating Pace of AI Development
- The AI labs' strategy of focusing on making AI great at writing code first has unlocked the potential for AI to improve itself, and this has led to rapid advancements in the field, with the current models being unrecognizable from those that existed even six months ago.
- The author believes that the experience of tech workers, who have seen AI go from being a helpful tool to being able to do their jobs better than they can, is about to be replicated in other industries, including law, finance, medicine, and more, with some experts predicting that this will happen in as little as one to five years.
- The author notes that many people's perceptions of AI are based on outdated experiences, and that the current models are far more advanced than what was available even a year ago, with the free versions of AI tools being over a year behind what paying users have access to.
- The gap between public perception and current reality is significant, and the author is concerned that this gap is preventing people from preparing for the impact that AI will have on their industries and jobs, with some experts, such as partners at major law firms, already seeing the potential of AI to revolutionize their work.
- The author's friend, a lawyer, is skeptical about the use of AI in his field, but the author believes that AI has the potential to greatly improve the work of lawyers and other professionals, and that those who are not preparing for this change are at risk of being left behind.

## AI's Capabilities and Self-Improvement Mechanisms
- The managing partner of a company, with decades of experience, is using AI for his work and has noticed that it becomes significantly more capable every couple of months, to the point where he expects it to be able to do most of what he does before long.
- The pace of improvement in AI is rapid, with notable advancements in recent years, including the ability to do basic arithmetic reliably in 2023, pass the bar exam in 2023, write working software and explain graduate-level science in 2024, and handle most coding work for top engineers by late 2025.
- The organization [[METR]] tracks the length of real-world tasks that AI models can complete successfully without human help, and their data shows that this duration has been doubling approximately every seven months, with recent data suggesting it may be accelerating to as fast as every four months.
- According to METR's measurements, the latest AI model, [[Claude (language model) | Claude]] Opus 4.5, can complete tasks that take a human expert nearly five hours, and this number is expected to continue growing, potentially leading to AI that can work independently for days within the next year, weeks within two years, and month-long projects within three years.
- [[Dario Amodei]], the CEO of [[Anthropic]], has stated that AI models "substantially smarter than almost all humans at almost all tasks" are on track for 2026 or 2027, which could have significant implications for various jobs, including office work.
- A recent development is that AI is now building the next AI, with OpenAI's GPT-5.3 Codex being the first model that was instrumental in creating itself, using early versions to debug its own training, manage its own deployment, and diagnose test results and evaluations.
- This creates a feedback loop between current AI and next-generation AI, which is gathering steam month by month, and could lead to an intelligence explosion, where each generation of AI helps build the next, smarter one, potentially leading to massive disruption in the job market.

## Impact of AI on Jobs and Industries
- Dario Amodei has predicted that AI will eliminate 50% of entry-level white-collar jobs within one to five years, and many people in the industry think this prediction may be conservative, given the capabilities of the latest AI models.
- The current wave of automation, driven by Artificial Intelligence (AI), is different from previous waves as it is a general substitute for cognitive work, improving simultaneously in various tasks, and leaving no convenient gap for workers to move into after being displaced.
- AI is affecting almost all knowledge work, including legal work, such as reading contracts, summarizing case law, drafting briefs, and doing legal research, with the potential to outperform junior associates.
- Financial analysis, writing and content creation, software engineering, medical analysis, and customer service are also being impacted by AI, with the technology handling complex tasks competently and improving rapidly.
- The author, who has expertise in software engineering, notes that AI can now write hundreds of thousands of lines of code correctly, automating large parts of the job, and predicts that there will be far fewer programming roles in the future.
- AI is also approaching or exceeding human performance in areas such as medical analysis, and is being used for emotional support, advice, and companionship, with the trend expected to grow.
- The author believes that nothing that can be done on a computer is safe in the medium term, and that if a job involves reading, writing, analyzing, deciding, or communicating through a keyboard, AI is likely to take over significant parts of it.

## Strategies for Leveraging AI Effectively
- To stay ahead, the author recommends starting to use AI seriously, signing up for paid versions of AI tools like [[Claude (language model) | Claude]] or [[ChatGPT]], and using the best model available, such as GPT-5.2 or Claude Opus 4.6, and following experts like Matt Shumer on X (@mattshumer_) to stay current on the latest developments.
- The author emphasizes the importance of not just asking AI quick questions, but rather using it to its full potential, and notes that being early to understand and adapt to AI is a significant advantage.
- The key to getting ahead with AI is to actively look for ways to automate parts of your job that used to take hours, rather than just using it casually for quick research questions or simple tasks.
- To maximize the benefits of AI, start by identifying the tasks that take up the most time and see how AI can be used to streamline or automate them, and be willing to iterate and refine your approach as needed.
- It is essential to remember that AI is rapidly improving, and even if it is not perfect today, it will likely be much better in six months, so it is crucial to start learning and using these tools now to gain a competitive advantage.

## Preparing for AI-Driven Disruption
- The person who can demonstrate the ability to use AI to analyze data, draft documents, or perform other tasks quickly and efficiently will be highly valued in their organization, and this can be a significant opportunity for career advancement.
- However, it is also important to be aware of the potential disruptions that AI can bring to various industries, and to take steps to build financial resilience, such as saving money, being cautious about taking on debt, and having a flexible budget.
- Certain roles, such as those that require physical presence, licensed accountability, or heavy regulatory compliance, may be less susceptible to disruption by AI, at least in the short term, but it is still essential to be adaptable and open to learning new skills.
- When it comes to advising the next generation, it is crucial to rethink the traditional playbook and focus on teaching kids how to work with AI, be curious, adaptable, and passionate about their pursuits, rather than just optimizing for a specific career path.
- Ultimately, the ability to work effectively with AI and be open to learning and adapting will be critical for success in the future, and those who can do so will have a significant advantage in the job market.

## The Democratization of AI Tools and Knowledge
- The barrier to building something, such as an app or writing a book, has largely been removed due to the availability of artificial intelligence (AI) that can assist with these tasks, allowing individuals to describe their ideas and have a working version in a short amount of time.
- With AI, knowledge is essentially free, and the tools to build things are extremely cheap, making it possible for people to pursue their passions and interests without being limited by technical skills or financial resources.
- The ability to adapt and learn new skills quickly is becoming increasingly important, as AI is constantly changing and improving, and the models that exist today will likely be obsolete in a year, requiring individuals to be comfortable with the pace of change and to continually experiment with new tools and technologies.

## Risks, Benefits, and Ethical Considerations
- Amodei's thought experiment, which imagines a new country appearing overnight with 50 million citizens who are smarter and faster than any human, highlights the potential risks and benefits of AI, with the possibility of solving complex medical problems, but also posing significant national security threats if not handled properly.
- The creators of AI technology, such as those at [[Anthropic]], are both excited and frightened by its potential, and are working to develop AI that can be controlled and used for the betterment of society, while also acknowledging the risks of AI that can behave in unpredictable ways or be used for malicious purposes.

## Future Outlook and Urgency for Adaptation
- The next two to five years are expected to be disorienting, with significant changes and advancements in AI technology, and individuals who are able to adapt and learn quickly will be better positioned to take advantage of these changes and to mitigate the potential risks associated with AI.
- Spending one hour a day experimenting with AI and trying new things can help individuals understand what's coming and stay ahead of the curve, with the potential to gain a significant advantage over others who are not taking the time to learn about and adapt to these new technologies.
- The people who will come out of the current situation best are those who start engaging now with curiosity and a sense of urgency, rather than fear, and it is essential to hear this information from someone who cares about them before it's too late.
- The future is already here, but it has not yet affected everyone, and it is crucial to start thinking about it now, rather than waiting for it to become a reality, at which point it may be too late to get ahead of it.
- It is important to share this information with others who should be thinking about the future, as most people will not hear about it until it is too late, and by sharing it, one can give someone they care about a head start.
